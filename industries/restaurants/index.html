Common Problems We See in Restaurants

Restaurant owners are some of the hardest-working people we know. The problem isn't effort — it's that the business infrastructure hasn't kept up with the growth.

  • Thin margins with no clear visibility into what's causing them
  • Multi-location complexity — each location tracks things differently
  • Labor costs eating into profit with no structured scheduling or hiring process
  • No consolidated financial view across locations
  • The owner is the only one who knows how things really work
  • Lease terms, vendor contracts, and expenses scattered everywhere

Most restaurant owners opened a second or third location because the first one was doing well. But scaling a restaurant isn't just about replicating the food — it's about replicating the systems. Without standardized financial tracking, documented processes, and a structured approach to hiring, each new location adds complexity without adding clarity.

The result: you're working harder, your margins haven't improved, and you can't tell which location is actually making money.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can a business consultant help a restaurant with thin margins?

Thin margins usually mean there's no financial visibility — you don't know your real food cost by item, your labor cost is reactive, and you can't see P&L by location. We help you get the financial infrastructure in place so you can make decisions based on actual numbers instead of guessing.

Do you work with multi-location restaurant owners?

Yes. Multi-location complexity is one of the most common reasons restaurant owners reach out to us. When each location tracks things differently and there's no consolidated view, it's impossible to make good decisions. We standardize tracking, build cross-location dashboards, and create the systems that let you manage by the numbers.

Can you help with restaurant staffing issues?

We help build a hiring system that's proactive instead of reactive — structured job descriptions, sourcing strategies, interview processes, and onboarding checklists. We can't solve the labor market, but we can make sure you're not losing good candidates to a disorganized process.

What's the first thing you typically fix for restaurant clients?

Financial organization. If you can't see your real numbers — food cost, labor cost, P&L by location — everything else is guesswork. Clean financials are the foundation for every other decision.
